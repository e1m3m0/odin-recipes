La Bandera Dominicana

La Bandera Dominicana is the tricolor Dominican flag with its distinctive red and blue opposing quarters, divided by a white cross.

Its namesake, La Bandera Dominicana food, is the Dominican national lunch meal. The red is represented by the beans, the white by the rice, and – with a splash of poetic and culinary license here – the meat – usually chicken or beef – represents the third color.

Making La Bandera

This plate is a combination of following dishes:
  • Rice: White rice is the base of la Bandera, and a staple of most Dominican lunch meals.
  • Beans: Stewed beans are served as part of it, usually red kidney beans, pinto beans, or cranberry beans.
  • Meat: The most commonly found is braised chicken or beef.
  • Salad: There's always also Dominican salad to go with them.

Serving La Bandera

  1. We start with the base white rice.
  2. The beans go second, and can be served over or next to rice.
  3. The meat is served next. In some dominican homes visitor get their meat server on the separate plate as a form of courtesy.
  4. Based on the occasion and family social status, La Bandera is serve with a green salad and other add ons (like fried platain, slice avocado, rippen banana, and many other).
